Eiffel Tower - Paris

Rising 330 m above the Seine, the Eiffel Tower isn't just Paris's postcard image , it's a working monument with restaurants, glass-floored walkways and sweeping views. Built as a temporary exhibit for the 1889 World's Fair, it became the city's permanent symbol of innovation and romance.

Colosseum - Rome

This massive amphitheatre, finished in 80 AD, once echoed with the roar of 50,000 spectators watching gladiators, wild animals and dramas of imperial power. Today, you can wander its tiers and underground passages to feel the scale of ancient Rome up close.

Statue of Liberty - New York

Standing at the mouth of New York Harbor, the Statue of Liberty was gifted by France in 1886 as a beacon of freedom and new beginnings. Ferries take visitors to Liberty Island, where a museum explains the statue's creation and you can climb up to the crown for a skyline view.
